Bug fix: after the clear-cache + fast-initial-scan test, Live Photos were phantom-flagging their paired motion video as a deletion candidate (visible as "Aging out" entries in Pending Review even though nothing had actually been deleted on the phone).

Cause: a Live Photo is one PHAsset localId on the phone but two server assets on Immich (still photo + motion video, linked by livePhotoVideoId). Imputation seeded only the still's checksum, so when the hash pass skipped the asset (modDate matched the imputed row), the motion video's checksum was missing from the local cache. ObservedStore retained it from prior syncs, the engine saw it as missing, and quarantine started.

The fix: imputation now seeds both checksums under the same localId by following the still's livePhotoVideoId to its paired motion asset. Users who already had phantom entries can dismiss or exclude them via Pending Review; the bug doesn't repeat from this build onward.

UI follow-ups in the same build:
- Sync detail's current-phase card now keeps a constant height during hashing so the timeline below it doesn't bounce every time the currently-hashed file changes
- Activity feed coalesces consecutive hash-progress entries into a single rolling row instead of flooding the list with hundreds of near-identical entries
- After the imputation pass, the activity feed gets a follow-up line breaking down WHY each part of the residue is falling through to local hashing ("N not on Immich, M ambiguous, …")
- Pending review's compact "Aging out" rows put the "eligible in N days" text on its own line so it doesn't truncate
