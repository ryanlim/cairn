Alive-on-phone safety: ±1 second tolerance on the capture-time match.

The user-reported still-flagged video confirmed on phone + Immich with matching filename and (visibly) matching timestamp turned out NOT to be a Live Photo — a standalone minutes-long .MOV. That ruled out the build-117 paired-motion-video fix and left rounded-second divergence between phone and server as the most plausible remaining mechanism:

- Phone reads `PHAsset.creationDate` directly, full precision.
- Server's `fileCreatedAt` round-trips through ISO8601 + JSON encoding to get to cairn; somewhere in that chain (Immich's mobile uploader, the Postgres timestamp column's precision setting, the SDK's date encoder) a fractional-second value could have been rounded.
- Both sides truncate `Date.timeIntervalSince1970` via `Int(...)`, which truncates toward zero. If one side has `.999` and the other has `.000` after the round, the integer seconds differ by 1.
- Exact-match `Set.contains` lookup misses, asset slips into `wouldBeCandidates`.

This build widens the engine's lookup to check `[baseSecond - 1, baseSecond, baseSecond + 1]` against the alive-key set. Three set lookups per server-asset candidate instead of one; negligible overhead (set lookups are O(1) and there are O(thousands) of candidates max).

False-positive risk: two distinct phone assets sharing both their filename AND an adjacent capture-second. Realistically near-zero — iPhone Camera doesn't produce same-named files in adjacent seconds, and user-imported files don't typically cluster that tightly. The safety direction (suppress more) favors widening here.

3 new engine test cases cover the tolerance: `+1s match` (suppress), `-1s match` (suppress), `+2s no match` (still a candidate; tolerance is bounded). Full 667 test suite passes including the new cases.

Combined with builds 115 (case + metadata-store source), 116 (broadened source types + hidden), and 117 (Live Photo paired motion video), this rounds out the divergence shapes we've observed in real end-user reports.
