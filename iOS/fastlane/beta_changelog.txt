Smoother navigation push from Settings search results.

Tapping a search result was showing a visible "partial render → brief lag → full populate" stagger on its way into the destination sub-page. Closure-form NavigationLinks interleave destination body computation with the push animation; on a sub-page with several KeylineSection cards full of sliders + HelpPopovers (Library, Safety & limits), that's enough work to break the animation budget — especially while the .searchable search-bar dismiss is running concurrently.

This build switches the search-result rows to value-based navigation (`NavigationLink(value: SettingsPage.x)` paired with a NavigationStack-level `.navigationDestination(for:)`). SwiftUI builds the destination off the animation critical path, so the push reads as smooth. Root-list rows are unchanged — they didn't exhibit the lag and the destination view is identical either way.
