Inline incremental-sync hashing now surfaces progress in the UI when the batch is large.

End-user comment on the build-125 "hang" (which was actually 524 untracked-sweep assets iCloud-fetching for minutes): "It also didn't surface any of that in the UI, which is non-ideal."

Right — and not just for that 125-specific case. Any time an incremental sync gets a batch of 50+ inserts/updates (untracked-sweep, mass iCloud Photos catch-up, returning from a long suspension where many phones arrived), the hashing was silent. The `hashAssets(ids:)` overload that handles inline incremental hashing in `runIncremental`'s inserted path was calling the underlying `hashAssets(assets:)` without `reportProgressWithTotal`. That meant `onHashProgress` never fired, `syncProgress` stayed nil, and the user saw "Syncing..." for minutes with no indication of work.

This build threads `reportProgressWithTotal` through the `(ids:)` overload when the batch is at or above 50 assets. Smaller batches (the typical incremental sync with a handful of inserts) still suppress reporting — the syncProgress churn isn't worth it for work that completes in a second or two. The 50-asset threshold is conservative and easy to revisit if it turns out the right line is higher or lower.

Behavior at the threshold:
- ≤49 assets: silent inline hashing (current behavior, fine for typical incremental syncs)
- ≥50 assets: `onHashProgress(done, total, [])` fires every 25 assets or 250 ms, populates `syncProgress`, surfaces in whatever UI is showing the in-flight sync — Status banner counters, anywhere `model.syncProgress.hashed/total` is read

No engine, no reconciliation, no behavioral change beyond the progress callback wiring. Just makes the UI honest about how much work is in flight.
